3D Principles

11 June 2021 • Technology & cool stuff •

3D printing is based on additive manufacturing, where an object is designed on a computer thanks to specialised prograam and then divided into separate layers, that will be printed on top of each other. We can imagine this process easily, think about printing a book. Each page is a separate layer, then we stack them and bind together to form a whole product. The difference is that book "layers" are just rectangles, while in 3D printing those layers can have diferent shapes. Quality of our object depends on the amout of layers building it - more is better! When it comes to the materials, as long as it can be easily heated to create moldable mass and then harden quickly it can be used in 3D priting, for example chocolate, concrete, powdered metals, thermoplastic resins.
